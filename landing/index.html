FoxVox is an open-source Chrome extension powered by GPT-4, that we created to demonstrate how AI can be used for spreading disinformation and subverting the content of respected online media.

Why is this dangerous?

  • Siloed realities:Companies, governments, and cyber criminals already know a lot about you. Cookies track your internet activity, data brokers buy and sell detailed demographic info, and companies pay hundreds of billions of dollars for this data to effectively target their advertising. In the past, this data could influence which human-written content lands in your newsfeed. But now…

    News providers, marketers, and political campaigns could rewrite articles on the fly that are tailored to the age of your children, your political preferences or the medical conditions. You’d never even know you were shown content that had been optimized to conform to your worldview.

  • Propaganda: Your perception of reality can be subtly manipulated without your knowledge. Today, companies increasingly use to generate, analyze and structure their data.
  • Hidden biases: The world can be subtly manipulated without anybody’s knowledge. Today, companies increasingly use AI with consistent political biases to generate, analyze and structure their data.
  • Breakdown of trust: It is already difficult to know who to trust or what to believe on the internet. The cumulative effects of ubiquitous AI-generated content could lead to the further breakdown of institutional trust and increased polarization. If people are overwhelmed with targeted AI content, foundations of online public discourse and informed decision making would erode.

FoxVox is fast and cheap

It can process large pages like the NYT in less than 10 seconds, and can process hundreds of pages for less than $20. For those willing to wait longer or pay more, the text quality can be significantly improved with chain/tree of thought and better context utilization./ToT and better context utilisation.
